The Bioscience Problem

Modeling Fruit Growth Dynamics for Harvest Precision

In precision agriculture, predicting fruit weight and understanding development rates is critical. The Malaysian **Harumanis Mango (clone MA 128)** undergoes three key growth phases post-anthesis: initial slow cell division, rapid cell expansion, and mature starch conversion (ripening).

By applying a Sigmoidal Logistic Growth Curve $f(t)$, we can map cumulative fresh weight over the standard 90-day post-flower-opening cycle. Its mathematical derivative, $f'(t)$, represents the instantaneous growth rate in grams per day ($\text{g/day}$). Analyzing where this rate peaks (the inflection point) allows agronomists to optimize fertigation schedules, Potassium (K) application, and pinpoint optimal harvest maturity window.

Growth Period 90 Days Post-Anthesis

Mathematical Formulation

Logistic Growth Equations

Cumulative Weight Model, $f(t)$:
$$f(t) = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(t - t_0)}}$$
Instantaneous Growth Rate, $f'(t)$ (Derivative):
$$f'(t) = \frac{dL}{dt} = \frac{L \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(t-t_0)}}{\left(1 + e^{-k(t-t_0)}\right)^2}$$
Where $L$ is the asymptotic mature weight, $k$ is the specific relative growth constant, $t_0$ is the midpoint of rapid growth, and $t$ represents Days After Anthesis (DAA).

Harumanis mango clone is primarily cultivated in Perlis. Recognized for its unique fragrance and high sweetness index. A premium agricultural product that is intensively researched by agricultural authorities in Malaysia.

Inflection Point Analysis

At $t = t_0$ (Inflection Point), the second derivative $f''(t) = 0$. This represents the absolute peak growth phase transition, where the acceleration of growth slows down, and fruit transitions to maturation.
